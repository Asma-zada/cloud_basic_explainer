## Cloud Basic Explainer Review_Notes
## What was improved
1. Simplified technical cloud terms into plain language a beginner can understand
2. Made instruction-response pairs consistent in length and tone
3. Expended 30 examples to cover more basic cloud concepts
4. Removed redundant questions and replaced them with distinct topics like AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, SaaS, IaaS, PaaS, serverless, cloud storage, virtual machine, cloud backup, cloud migration, auto-scalling, cloud hosting

## What mistakes were removed
1. Vague definitions that assumed prior knowledge
2. Responses that were too long or too technical for beginners
3. Duplicate coverage of the same concept with different wording
4. Inconsistent formatting in JSON lines

## What was learned 
1. Writing for beginners means avoiding jargon and using everyday examples
2. 30 diverse but simple question and answer pairs cover the "cloud basics" well without overwhelming the reader
3. Splitting data 80% train and 20% test work cleanly with 30 total examples: 24 for train and 6 for test
4. Keeping responses under 2 sentences improves clarity for new learners
